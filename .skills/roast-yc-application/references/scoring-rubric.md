# YC Application Scoring Rubric

Detailed criteria for evaluating each category. Use this to justify your scores.

---

## 1. Clarity & Conciseness (20 points)

### 18-20: Exceptional
- Idea explained in one clear sentence
- Zero jargon or buzzwords
- Every word serves a purpose
- "X for Y" format used effectively (e.g., "Airbnb for boats")
- Could be understood by a smart 12-year-old

### 14-17: Good
- Idea clear within first paragraph
- Minimal jargon (1-2 instances)
- Mostly concise with some fluff
- Minor redundancy

### 10-13: Needs Work
- Takes multiple paragraphs to understand
- Jargon present throughout
- Verbose explanations
- Reader has to work to extract meaning

### Below 10: Major Issues
- Unclear what the product actually does
- Heavy marketing speak
- Walls of text
- Buzzword soup ("AI-powered blockchain platform revolutionizing...")

### Red Flag Phrases (Deduct 2 points each):
- "Revolutionize" / "Disrupt"
- "Synergy" / "Leverage"
- "Best-in-class" / "World-class"
- "Paradigm shift"
- "End-to-end solution"
- "Seamless" / "Frictionless" (without specifics)

---

## 2. Founder Credibility (20 points)

### 18-20: Exceptional
- Specific, impressive achievements with numbers
- Relevant domain expertise demonstrated
- Prior startup experience or significant projects
- Top-tier credentials that directly apply
- Example: "Built payment system processing $2M/month at Stripe"

### 14-17: Good
- Clear accomplishments mentioned
- Some relevant background
- Solid technical or business credentials
- Missing some specificity

### 10-13: Needs Work
- Generic descriptions ("passionate entrepreneur")
- Credentials not clearly relevant
- Accomplishments vague or unquantified
- Heavy on adjectives, light on evidence

### Below 10: Major Issues
- No specific achievements mentioned
- Background doesn't relate to problem
- Only generic traits listed
- Reads like a resume summary

### What to Look For:
- Quantified achievements ("grew users from 0 to 10k")
- Relevant industry experience
- Technical capability demonstrated
- Evidence of resourcefulness/scrappiness
- "Hardest thing you've done" answer is genuinely hard

---

## 3. Problem & Solution (15 points)

### 13-15: Exceptional
- Problem is specific and painful
- Clear who has this problem
- Solution directly addresses root cause
- Unique insight or approach explained
- Defensibility articulated (why can't others copy?)

### 10-12: Good
- Problem clearly stated
- Solution makes sense
- Some differentiation mentioned
- Missing deeper insight

### 7-9: Needs Work
- Problem too broad or vague
- Solution is obvious/commodity
- No clear differentiation
- "Solution looking for a problem" vibes

### Below 7: Major Issues
- Problem not clearly defined
- Solution doesn't match problem
- No explanation of why this approach
- Competitors ignored entirely

### Key Questions to Answer:
- Why does this problem exist?
- Why hasn't it been solved?
- What do you understand that others don't?
- Why will you win vs. incumbents?

---

## 4. Traction & Progress (15 points)

### 13-15: Exceptional
- Revenue (any amount shows validation)
- Active users with retention metrics
- Growth rate mentioned (week-over-week ideal)
- Waitlist with conversion data
- LOIs or pilot customers

### 10-12: Good
- Working product/MVP
- Some users (even if small)
- Beta feedback incorporated
- Clear progress timeline

### 7-9: Needs Work
- Only a prototype/mockup
- "Interested" users but no signups
- No metrics provided
- Unclear what's been built

### Below 7: Major Issues
- Idea stage only
- No product built
- "We plan to..." without evidence
- Months of work with nothing to show

### Traction Hierarchy (best to worst):
1. Profitable revenue
2. Revenue
3. Paid users
4. Active unpaid users
5. Signups/waitlist
6. Letters of intent
7. User interviews
8. Nothing

---

## 5. Market Understanding (10 points)

### 9-10: Exceptional
- Bottom-up market sizing with real numbers
- Specific competitor analysis
- Clear wedge/entry strategy
- Expansion path articulated

### 7-8: Good
- Reasonable market size estimate
- Main competitors acknowledged
- Some differentiation explained

### 5-6: Needs Work
- Top-down sizing only ("$50B market")
- Competitors mentioned but not analyzed
- Generic positioning

### Below 5: Major Issues
- "No competitors" (always a red flag)
- Unrealistic market claims
- No go-to-market thinking
- "If we capture 1% of..." reasoning

### Market Size Red Flags:
- TAM without SAM/SOM
- Only top-down calculations
- Claiming "no competition"
- Numbers without sources

---

## 6. Team Dynamics (10 points)

### 9-10: Exceptional
- 2-3 co-founders with complementary skills
- Long history working together
- Clear role division
- Equal or fair equity split
- Full-time commitment

### 7-8: Good
- Multiple founders
- Skills complement each other
- Reasonable working history
- Committed to the venture

### 5-6: Needs Work
- Solo founder (not disqualifying but harder)
- Skills overlap significantly
- New team, unproven together
- Part-time involvement

### Below 5: Major Issues
- Solo founder with no plan to find co-founder
- Team seems mismatched
- Unclear commitment level
- Red flags about stability

### Team Questions:
- How long have you worked together?
- How do you resolve disagreements?
- What's the equity split?
- Are you all full-time?

---

## 7. Video Quality (5 points)

### 5: Exceptional
- Under 1 minute
- Opens with the problem
- Founders speak naturally (not scripted)
- No fancy production (authenticity)
- Energy and conviction visible

### 3-4: Good
- Reasonable length
- Covers key points
- Founders present
- Minor issues (too long, slightly scripted)

### 1-2: Needs Work
- Too long or too short
- Scripted/robotic delivery
- Missing key information
- Poor audio/video quality

### 0: Missing or Major Issues
- No video submitted
- Only one founder speaks
- Completely off-topic
- Professional ad instead of authentic pitch

### Video Tips:
- Look at the camera
- State the problem in first 10 seconds
- Show the product if possible
- Be yourselves

---

## 8. X-Factor (5 points)

### 5: Exceptional
- Memorable hook or story
- Evidence of "hacker" mindset
- Creative problem-solving demonstrated
- Something that makes you say "wow"
- Unconventional path or insight

### 3-4: Good
- Interesting angle
- Some personality showing
- Decent "why you" answer

### 1-2: Needs Work
- Generic application
- Nothing memorable
- Reads like everyone else

### 0: Missing
- Completely forgettable
- No personality
- Cookie-cutter responses

### What Creates X-Factor:
- A specific story about discovering the problem
- An unusual background that gives unique insight
- Evidence of extreme resourcefulness
- Something you've "hacked" or figured out creatively
- Genuine passion that comes through

---

## Final Score Interpretation

| Score | Verdict | Recommendation |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| 90-100 | Interview likely | Submit with confidence |
| 80-89 | Strong application | Minor polish, then submit |
| 70-79 | Competitive | Address feedback, improve traction |
| 60-69 | Needs work | Significant revisions needed |
| 50-59 | Weak | Major rewrites required |
| Below 50 | Not ready | Reconsider approach entirely |

---

## Quick Reference: YC's Own Advice

From YC's official "How to Apply" page:

1. **Be clear** - "Lack of clarity is not only confusing, it makes you look slippery."
2. **Be concrete** - Specific > vague always
3. **Answer the question** - Don't evade or redirect
4. **Be genuine** - They've read 10,000 applications; they spot BS
5. **Show, don't tell** - Evidence > claims
